Introduction

Welcome — my name is Jonah Smith, and I’m a senior at the University of Pittsburgh studying Computer Science, Data Science, and Applied Statistics. I’m passionate about using data, algorithms, and experimentation to solve real-world problems, especially in technology, financial markets, and digital intelligence.

My academic journey grew out of a challenge familiar to many students: balancing a demanding STEM curriculum while trying to build meaningful experience early in my career. Instead of treating coursework and personal projects as separate tracks, I merged them — turning class concepts into working models, research replications, and applied software tools. This approach helped me grow technically while strengthening my interest in quantitative work, machine learning, and financial modeling.

Professionally, I’ve built data systems and automation pipelines as a Software Developer and Data Analyst Intern at Federal Specialized Services, developing tools for large-scale data processing, legal/regulatory workflows, and internal reporting. These opportunities allowed me to apply my programming and analytical skills in real environments and reinforced my interest in building systems that help people make better decisions.

Looking ahead, I believe learning compounds — every project teaches something that opens the door to the next. Becoming skilled in any domain takes patience, curiosity, and deliberate practice, and that philosophy guides how I approach my academic, professional, and personal development. Through this portfolio, I share a snapshot of my work and the foundation I’m continuing to build as I work toward a career at the intersection of computing, analytics, and problem-solving.

General Professional Cover Letter

Dear Reader,

Thank you for taking the time to explore my professional portfolio. My name is Jonah Smith, and I am a senior at the University of Pittsburgh pursuing a double major in Computer Science and Data Science, with a minor in Applied Statistics. My academic work, personal projects, and professional experiences all stem from a simple motivation: I enjoy using data, computation, and experimentation to understand the world — and then improve it.

Throughout my undergraduate years, I’ve built a path defined by both discipline and curiosity. Balancing a demanding course load with research, software development, and personal passion projects has taught me how to adapt, prioritize, and build momentum under pressure. Instead of choosing between academic rigor and independent learning, I combined the two — transforming coursework concepts into personal experiments in markets, equities, synthetic data modeling, and long-term machine-learning research replication projects.

Professionally, I’ve applied these skills as a Software Developer and Data Analyst Intern at Federal Specialized Services, where I built and deployed tools that supported regulatory work, automated complex data pipelines, and assisted attorneys by developing conditional data extraction systems. These experiences strengthened my foundation in Python, SQL, data engineering, and analytical reasoning while reinforcing my passion for designing tools that streamline decision-making.

Outside the classroom, I maintain strong personal interests in financial markets, quantitative investing, music, and strength training. I’ve been playing guitar for over three years and enjoy learning new songs and improvising in my free time. Powerlifting is another major part of my life, and I love the process of pushing myself, setting goals, and seeing steady progress over time.

As I continue advancing into the professional world, I aim to build on these experiences — leveraging my statistical background, computational skills, and appetite for learning to contribute to data-driven teams and impactful technology. I am committed to continuous growth, and I look forward to building meaningful work in the years ahead.

Sincerely,
Jonah Smith
